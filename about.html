About Solar Wind Monitor

Solar Wind Monitor is a real-time dashboard that displays various solar wind parameters and their effects on Earth's magnetosphere. This tool is designed to help space weather enthusiasts, researchers, and anyone interested in solar-terrestrial physics to monitor current space weather conditions.

The data displayed on this dashboard is sourced from various space weather monitoring services, including NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center (SWPC).

Parameters Monitored:

  • Bt/Bz: Interplanetary Magnetic Field components (Last 2 hours)
  • Solar Wind Density (Last 2 hours)
  • Solar Wind Speed (Last 2 hours)
  • Solar Wind Temperature (Last 2 hours)
  • Kyoto Dst Index (Last 12 hours)
  • Magnetometer readings (Last 6 hours)

For more information about space weather and its effects, please visit NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center.
